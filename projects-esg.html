    Overview of the System

    The ESG Scoring System is a key component of a two-part initiative aimed at incorporating Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) factors into the traditional credit scoring framework. This innovative approach seeks to provide a more well-rounded evaluation of a borrowerâ€™s creditworthiness by integrating sustainability metrics alongside financial data. The system focuses on three core pillars of ESG: Environmental, Social, and Governance. Each pillar is assessed using five Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), carefully chosen to reflect critical aspects of sustainable practices. These KPIs form the foundation for ESG ratings, enabling Development Financing Institutions (DFIs) to benchmark and improve their sustainability performance.

    Presentation of the System to the Public

    The ESG Scoring System was officially introduced to the public at the ADFIAP International CEO Forum in Bali, Indonesia. This prestigious event brought together key leaders from the finance and sustainability sectors, offering a valuable platform for sharing innovative solutions for sustainable development. During the forum, the system was showcased as an advanced tool for evaluating the sustainability practices of financial institutions. The presentation included a comprehensive demonstration of the system's features, focusing on how it helps institutions track, measure, and improve their environmental, social, and governance performance. It also provided an excellent opportunity to highlight how the system could drive better decision-making and promote sustainability within the finance sector.

    My Role in the Project

    As the sole Data Analyst and Power BI Developer on the team, I played a pivotal role in a variety of tasks essential to the success of the project. My responsibilities included developing a framework and implementing methods to assess and measure the sustainability of Department Financing Institutions, ensuring the accuracy and relevance of the collected data. My contributions helped streamline the data collection process, ensuring that the final results were reliable and actionable.
