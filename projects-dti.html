    DTI Workshops

    The DTI Workshops was a 5-Day project aimed to address critical aspects of digital transformation and innovation in public and governmental services handled by the Department of Trade and Industry (DTI). Infivex, a technology solutions company, was tasked to handle the distribution of lessons and tasks given to the participants. Both workshops brought together experts, stakeholders, and government representatives to create actionable plans for improving consumer services and empowering microenterprises.

    Chatbot Design Workshop (2 Days)

    This workshop was conducted to address challenges in DTIâ€™s consumer complaint resolution process by designing an AI-powered chatbot system. The aim of the workshop was to find a method to streamline the complaint handling of DTI, enhance consumer accessibility, and provide real-time assistance through a virtual assistant capable of addressing frequently asked questions and managing complaints. The sessions included various activities for the participants to do such as process mapping, chatbot flow design conception, and system integration planning to ensure the future chatbot's functionality and effectiveness.

    BMBE Design and Development Workshop (3 Days)

    This workshop focused on improving the existing BMBE Online Registration System by investigating technical issues, enhancing user experience, and ensuring seamless integration with evolving business needs. The aim of the workshop was to redefine workflows, improve navigation and accessibility, and ensure the system's scalability and security. The workshop also covered possible designs of the new UI/UX of the registration system, identification of the functional and non-functional system requirements, and future architectural enhancements.

    My Role in the Project

    As the data analyst on the Infivex team, my role involved consolidating the workshop findings and transforming them into actionable insights. I was responsible for managing and proposing all data-related components of the future systems from flow diagraming to possible data management plans.
    In addition, I also assisted in the delivery of the workshop sessions and provided support to the participants.
